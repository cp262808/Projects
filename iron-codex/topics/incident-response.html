Incident Response

17 Controls Intermediate Level Operations

SIEM, log analysis, forensics, containment procedures, and recovery planning for effective cybersecurity incident management and response.

Overview

Incident response is the structured approach to addressing and managing the aftermath of a security breach or cyberattack. The goal is to handle the situation in a way that limits damage, reduces recovery time and costs, and enables the organization to maintain business operations while addressing the security incident.

Key Concepts Covered

Detection & Analysis

SIEM systems, log analysis, and threat hunting for early incident detection

Containment & Eradication

Rapid response procedures to contain threats and remove malicious presence

Recovery & Lessons Learned

System restoration and post-incident analysis for continuous improvement

Communication & Coordination

Stakeholder communication and coordination with internal and external teams

Preparation

Incident Response Plan

Develop comprehensive incident response procedures and playbooks.

Implementation:
  • Defined roles and responsibilities
  • Escalation procedures and contact lists

  • Communication templates and procedures

Incident Response Team

Establish and train dedicated incident response team members.

Tools and Resources

Detection & Analysis

Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

Implement SIEM for centralized log collection and correlation.

Implementation:
  • Log aggregation from all critical systems
  • Real-time correlation and alerting rules
  • Dashboard and reporting capabilities
  • Integration with threat intelligence feeds

Deploy network and host-based detection systems.

Log Analysis and Monitoring

Threat Hunting

Threat Eradication

NIST Incident Response Framework

1. Preparation

Establish incident response capability through planning, training, and resource allocation.

2. Detection & Analysis

Identify potential security incidents and analyze their scope and impact.

3. Containment, Eradication & Recovery

Contain the incident, eliminate the threat, and restore normal operations.

4. Post-Incident Activity

Document lessons learned and improve incident response processes.
