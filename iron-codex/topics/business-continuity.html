Business Continuity

12 Controls Beginner Level Process

Disaster recovery, backup strategies, RTO/RPO planning, resilience engineering, and crisis management for maintaining business operations during disruptions.

Overview

Business continuity ensures that an organization can continue operating during and after disruptive events. It encompasses disaster recovery, crisis management, and resilience planning to minimize downtime, protect critical business functions, and maintain stakeholder confidence during emergencies, natural disasters, cyberattacks, or other significant disruptions.

Key Concepts Covered

Disaster Recovery

Technical recovery of IT systems and infrastructure after disruptive events

Business Impact Analysis

Assessment of critical business functions and recovery time objectives

Crisis Management

Coordinated response to manage communications and stakeholder relationships

Resilience Planning

Building organizational resilience and adaptive capacity for future disruptions

RTO vs RPO Understanding

Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

Definition: Maximum acceptable time to restore business functions after disruption

Examples:

  • Critical systems: 4 hours
  • Important systems: 24 hours
  • Non-critical systems: 72 hours

Recovery Point Objective (RPO)

Definition: Maximum acceptable data loss measured in time from last backup

Examples:

  • Financial data: 15 minutes
  • Customer data: 1 hour
  • Archive data: 24 hours
