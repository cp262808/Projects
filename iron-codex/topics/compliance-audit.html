Compliance & Audit

15 Controls Intermediate Level Governance

SOX, PCI-DSS, HIPAA, ISO 27001, audit procedures, and regulatory framework implementation for comprehensive compliance management.

Overview

Compliance and audit management ensures organizations meet regulatory requirements, industry standards, and internal policies. This involves establishing governance frameworks, implementing controls, conducting assessments, and maintaining evidence of compliance to protect against legal, financial, and reputational risks.

Key Concepts Covered

Regulatory Frameworks

Understanding and implementing various regulatory requirements and industry standards

Control Implementation

Designing and implementing security controls to meet compliance requirements

Audit Management

Preparing for and managing internal and external audits and assessments

Continuous Monitoring

Ongoing monitoring and measurement of compliance status and control effectiveness

Key Compliance Frameworks

SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley)

Financial reporting and internal controls for public companies.

  • Section 302: CEO/CFO certification
  • Section 404: Internal control assessment
  • IT general controls and application controls
  • Change management and access controls

PCI-DSS

Payment card industry data security standards.

  • 12 requirements across 6 control objectives
  • Network security and access controls
  • Cardholder data protection
  • Regular testing and monitoring

HIPAA

Healthcare information privacy and security requirements.

  • Administrative safeguards
  • Physical safeguards
  • Technical safeguards
  • Breach notification requirements

ISO 27001

International standard for information security management.

  • 14 control domains with 114 controls
  • Risk-based approach
  • ISMS implementation and certification
  • Continuous improvement process

GDPR

European data protection and privacy regulation.

  • Data subject rights and consent
  • Privacy by design principles
  • Data protection impact assessments
  • Breach notification requirements

SOC 2

Service organization controls for security and availability.

  • Trust services criteria
  • Type I and Type II reports
  • Common criteria implementation
  • Third-party assurance
